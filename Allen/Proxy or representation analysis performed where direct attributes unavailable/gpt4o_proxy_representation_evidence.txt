## 3. Analysis of Proxy / Representation Dimensions

### What Counts as a Proxy Attribute

A proxy attribute is a variable that, while not directly a protected attribute, is correlated with it and can serve as a stand-in. For example, ZIP code is often used as a proxy for race due to historical and socio-economic factors that lead to racial segregation in housing. Similarly, browsing history might serve as a proxy for gender based on stereotypical patterns of online behavior.

**Verbatim Evidence:**
- "A proxy attribute is a variable that is correlated with a protected attribute, such as using ZIP code as a proxy for race." (Barocas et al., p. 35)

### Representation Bias and Proxy Discrimination

Representation bias occurs when certain groups are underrepresented or misrepresented in the data, leading to biased outcomes. Proxy discrimination arises when these biases are encoded through proxy attributes, resulting in unfair treatment of individuals based on these indirect correlations.

**Verbatim Evidence:**
- "Proxy attributes can inadvertently encode biases present in the data, leading to systemic discrimination." (NIST SP 1270, p. 14)

### Methods for Detecting Proxy Effects

Several methods are used to detect proxy effects, including:

- **Causal Graphs:** These are used to model the relationships between variables and identify proxy pathways that may lead to discrimination.
- **Correlation Tests:** Statistical tests to identify correlations between proxy attributes and protected attributes.
- **Latent Variables:** Techniques to infer hidden variables that may act as proxies.

**Verbatim Evidence:**
- "We define a causal graph to model the relationships between variables, allowing us to identify proxy pathways that may lead to discrimination." (Kusner et al., p. 2)

### Risks of Indirect Inference of Protected Attributes

Indirect inference of protected attributes through proxies can lead to several risks, including:

- **Unintentional Discrimination:** Systems may inadvertently discriminate against individuals based on proxy attributes.
- **Perpetuation of Historical Biases:** Proxy attributes can encode historical biases, leading to systemic discrimination.

**Verbatim Evidence:**
- "Proxy attributes can inadvertently encode biases present in the data, leading to systemic discrimination." (NIST SP 1270, p. 14)

### Documented Harms of Proxy-Based or Representational Bias

The harms associated with proxy-based or representational bias include:

- **Unfair Outcomes:** Individuals may receive unfair treatment based on proxy attributes rather than their actual characteristics.
- **Loss of Trust:** Users may lose trust in AI systems that exhibit biased behavior.
- **Legal and Ethical Concerns:** Organizations may face legal and ethical challenges if their systems are found to discriminate based on proxy attributes.

**Verbatim Evidence:**
- "Encodes societal biases, leading to unfair outcomes." (Barocas et al., p. 35)

## 4. Identified Gaps / Missing Disclosures

While the sources provide comprehensive insights into proxy attributes and representation bias, there are notable gaps:
